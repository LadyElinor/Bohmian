## Working title
**Governed Falsification and Causality Attribution in Minisuperspace Quantum-Correction Toy Models**

## Abstract (draft)
We present a governance-first methodology for evaluating speculative quantum-cosmology toy models under strict reproducibility and anti-overclaim controls. The testbed is an FRW-inspired minisuperspace ODE system with two correction lanes: additive \(\alpha_{qg}\) and a Bohmian quantum-potential lane (multiple \(Q\) forms). We apply a shoulder-causality protocol with four stages: numerical falsification, form falsification, dense local mapping, and decision gating. Across independent diagnostics, failures cluster near \(\Omega_m\approx 0.307\text{–}0.315\). In confirmatory runs, shoulder location remains pinned near \(\Omega_m\approx0.307\), invariance is low (\(\sim0.636\)), and form-shift is null (\(\Delta\Omega\approx0\)), yielding Intrinsic attribution at confidence 0.81 for current ansatz families. We make no broad physical or observational claim; the contribution is a reproducible governance framework that distinguishes numerical fragility from structural envelope limits and yields auditable decision artifacts.
